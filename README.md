# Indian-Tech-Jobs-Analysis
End to end analysis of identify hiring trends, salary patterns, skill demand, applicant competition, and recruitment behavior.

# PROJECT SUMARRY
This project analyzes the Indian technology job market using 5,000 job postings across multiple cities, industries, company types, and experience levels. The objective was to identify hiring trends, salary patterns, skill demand, applicant competition, and recruitment behavior using Python-based data analytics techniques.

# INSIGHTS
1. MARKET COMPETITION IS EXTREMELY HIGH-> The Indian tech job market demonstrates extremely high competition, with nearly 83 applicants competing for every available opening. This indicates a highly saturated talent market, particularly for general technical roles.
   # Recommendations-> Candidates should focus on niche and high-value technical skills to differentiate themselves in an increasingly competitive hiring environment.

2. EXPERIENCE HAS MASSIVE IMPACT ON SALARY-> Salary growth in the Indian tech sector scales aggressively with experience. Lead-level professionals earn nearly 10x more than freshers, reflecting strong market demand for experienced technical expertise.
   # Recommendations-> Professionals should prioritize continuous upskilling and specialization early in their careers to accelerate long-term salary growth.

3. SOFTWARE ENGINEERING ROLES DOMINATE HIRING->Core software engineering and development roles continue to dominate India’s technology hiring ecosystem, indicating sustained enterprise demand for scalable application development talent.

4. SOME ROLES ATTRACT EXTREME COMPETITION-> High-volume technical roles attract disproportionately large applicant pools, making recruitment highly competitive for candidates despite strong hiring demand.
   # Recommendations-> Candidates applying for mainstream technical roles should strengthen portfolios, certifications, and practical project experience to improve visibility.

5. AI AND ML ROLES OFFER THE HIGHEST SALARIES->AI and machine learning-related roles command the highest compensation levels in the Indian technology market, reflecting increasing enterprise investment in advanced analytics and artificial intelligence capabilities.
   # Recommendations-> Professionals aiming for premium compensation should invest in AI, machine learning, NLP, and computer vision skillsets.

6. COMPANY TYPE STRONGLY AFFECTS SALARY-> Indian Unicorns and startups offer significantly higher average salaries compared to traditional MNCs and government organizations, likely driven by aggressive growth strategies and specialized talent acquisition.
   # Recommendations-> Candidates seeking higher compensation and rapid career growth may benefit from targeting startup and unicorn ecosystems.
   startups → high salary
   MNCs → high stability + high hiring volume

7. REMOTE WORK IS NOW MAINSTREAM-> Remote Jobs: 25.34%, Remote openings: 38% of all openings
8.  PYTHON IS THE MOST DEMANDED SKILL
9.  HIRING IS RELATIVELY CONSISTENT THROUGHOUT THE YEAR
